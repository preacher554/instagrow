# Instagram Content System Layer

## Purpose

The Content System Layer converts Research Layer evidence into Instagram-native content strategy, copy systems, briefs, calendars, and experiments.

This layer does **not** produce final edited visuals or videos. It defines what should be said, why it should work, how it should be packaged, and what creative direction the production layer should execute next.

```text
Research Layer
  ↓ evidence, pain language, competitor gaps, opportunity scores
Content System Layer
  ↓ pillars, hooks, copy, scripts, media briefs, weekly calendar, experiment cards
Creative Production Layer
  ↓ final assets, edits, covers, subtitles, exports
Publishing Layer
  ↓ posting/scheduling/generate-only packages
Analytics + Learning Layer
  ↓ metrics, postmortems, playbook updates
```

## Non-Negotiable Inputs

Before building content, collect or label as `assumption`:

1. Account status: zero/new, existing/active, or dormant/messy.
2. Brand Intelligence Card.
3. Research report or quick research notes.
4. Content objective: reach, saves, shares, trust, DM, lead, sale, retention.
5. Audience temperature: ice cold, cool, warm, hot, loyal.
6. Platform/format: Reels, carousel, Story, single image, caption, DM, email if relevant.
7. Offer or monetization path if conversion is involved.

If inputs are missing, do not block by default. Produce a useful draft with explicit assumptions and a `Missing Inputs` section.

## Seven Pillars

### 1. Content Pillar Strategy

Define the stable topic map that teaches Instagram what the account is about.

Required outputs:

- 3-5 primary content pillars.
- 1-3 supporting pillars if needed.
- audience stage per pillar.
- monetization relevance per pillar.
- example topics per pillar.
- forbidden drift topics that damage topic-cluster clarity.

Common GrowthForge/InstaGrow pillar families:

- Problem Awareness.
- Tactical Education.
- Framework / Systems Thinking.
- Proof / Case Study.
- Authority / Opinion.
- Offer / Conversion.
- Relationship / Behind the Scenes.

### 2. Ideation Engine

Turn research into content bets.

Sources:

- pain language from comments/forums/reviews.
- competitor winners and gaps.
- X/Twitter narrative tension.
- TikTok short-form hooks.
- YouTube depth topics.
- keyword/search intent.
- objections and buying friction.
- cultural or temporal moments.

Every idea should include:

- source signal.
- audience pain/desire.
- psychological mechanism.
- suggested format.
- expected metric.
- opportunity score or priority band.

Anti-generic rule:

```text
Generic idea → Evidence → Mechanism → Instagram package → Metric target → Experiment decision
```

### 3. Format & Packaging System

Choose the format based on job-to-be-done.

- Reels: discovery, retention, shareability, fast trust.
- Carousel: saves, depth, frameworks, checklists, structured education.
- Stories: warmth, interaction, DM, relationship maintenance.
- Static/single image: identity, proof, quote, statement, brand memory.
- Caption: SEO, nuance, trust, conversion, objection handling.
- DM: private conversion and relationship repair.

Format selection must consider:

- audience temperature.
- objective.
- complexity of idea.
- production effort.
- brand voice.
- experiment value.

### 4. MUSE Copywriting Engine

MUSE is the copywriting and scriptwriting subsystem inside the Content System Layer.

MUSE handles:

- Brand Voice DNA.
- audience temperature calibration.
- hook and retention design.
- copywriting framework selection.
- TTA/CTA architecture.
- variants and testing hypotheses.
- copy audit/autopsy/rewrite.
- platform-native copy adaptation.

Use `playbooks/muse-copywriting-engine.md` for the full strategy.

### 5. Media Direction System

At this layer, media is **direction**, not final production.

Content System should output:

- visual hook.
- shot concept.
- B-roll idea.
- text overlay lines.
- thumbnail/cover headline.
- carousel slide hierarchy.
- editing rhythm notes.
- audio direction.
- production constraints.

Creative Production Layer later turns this into actual visuals, video edits, covers, subtitles, exports, and QA.

Boundary rule:

```text
Content System says: what the asset should communicate and why.
Creative Production says: how to create and finish the asset.
```

### 6. Calendar & Cadence

Default weekly cadence unless account stage or resources require adjustment:

- 3-5 Reels/week.
- 2-3 carousels/week.
- Stories daily.
- 1-2 relationship/proof/static posts when useful.

Use Asia/Jakarta (WIB) as the default timezone. General test windows:

- Tuesday-Friday, 09:00-11:00 WIB.
- Tuesday-Friday, 14:00-16:00 WIB.
- evening relationship/Story testing when audience behavior supports it.

Important posts should include the 30-minute engagement window:

1. Engage with niche accounts 15 minutes before posting.
2. Publish.
3. Reply to comments for 30 minutes.
4. Engage with niche accounts 15 minutes after posting.
5. Share to Stories immediately.

### 7. Experiment & Learning Loop

Every meaningful content recommendation should become an experiment.

Minimum experiment fields:

- hypothesis.
- format.
- hook mechanism.
- audience temperature.
- primary TTA.
- expected metric.
- decision rule.
- result.
- next action: repeat, iterate, kill, or escalate.

Track weekly, not emotionally day-by-day.

## Research-to-Content Translation Protocol

Use this mapping when converting Research Layer outputs:

- Pain point → hook angle + PAS+ problem specificity.
- Audience exact words → caption vocabulary + text overlay language.
- Objection → carousel slide, Reel beat, or decision-stage caption.
- Competitor winner → mechanism to adapt, not copy.
- Competitor gap → differentiation angle.
- Trend signal → packaging option, only if brand-fit is strong.
- Keyword → Instagram SEO caption + alt text suggestion.
- Cultural signal → timing, metaphor, reference pool, or avoid flag.
- Monetization signal → offer bridge, DM trigger, proof requirement.

## Content System Output Package

For a normal content cycle, produce:

1. Content pillar map.
2. Scored idea backlog.
3. Hook bank additions.
4. 3-5 Reel briefs.
5. 2-3 carousel briefs.
6. 5-10 Story prompts.
7. caption/TTA package.
8. media direction notes.
9. weekly content calendar.
10. experiment cards.

## Quality Gate

Before sending content to Creative Production, verify:

- Niche/topic cluster is clear.
- Research signal is present or assumption is labeled.
- Audience temperature is identified.
- Objective and metric are explicit.
- Hook has a psychological mechanism.
- Copy has a save/share/trust/conversion reason.
- TTA feels earned, not pasted on.
- Media direction supports the idea.
- Brand voice is not generic.
- There is at least one testable variant or experiment.

## Common Failure Modes

1. **Making content from vibes, not evidence.** Fix: attach a source signal or label as assumption.
2. **Hard-selling to cold audience.** Fix: shift to relevance, problem recognition, or trust proof.
3. **Treating copy as only caption text.** Fix: include hook, overlay, script, CTA/TTA, and media direction.
4. **Copying competitor formats blindly.** Fix: identify mechanism, then repackage through brand Voice DNA.
5. **Skipping experiment design.** Fix: every strategic post gets a hypothesis and decision rule.
6. **Moving into visual production too early.** Fix: finish copy strategy and media brief first, then hand off to Creative Production.
